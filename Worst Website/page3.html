Research and inspiration

I looked at Ling's Cars and the sites on Brutalist Websites. Ling's Cars is packed with moving stuff, loud colors, weird buttons, and things fighting for your attention. The brutalist examples were useful because a lot of them ignore the normal clean website look and leave things rough, awkward, or way too direct.

My idea was to start with one simple button and ruin the page as the user keeps clicking it. The meter goes up, sounds stack together, messages appear, and the screen starts moving. I used old MLG meme colors and Rick and Morty GIFs because they fit the kind of loud internet humor I was going for. Page 2 is basically the point where I stopped pretending the layout was under control.

Design principle: repetition

I picked repetition. The same thick borders, warnings, fake rewards, bright colors, GIFs, and notifications keep showing up. Repetition is normally supposed to make a design feel consistent. I repeated everything too much so it turns into noise. The user is also repeating the same click over and over, so the interaction matches the design principle.

What I intentionally dislike

I hate sites where everything flashes, every button acts important, random things make noise, and pop-ups cover what I was trying to read. I also dislike fake countdowns, all-caps warnings, crooked alignment, and colors that hurt to look at together. I put all of that into this site on purpose. The navigation and sound controls still work, but almost everything else is trying to annoy the user.

stuff I used

  • Three connected pages with Home, Page 2, and Page 3 navigation
  • More than ten images across the three pages
  • Three different page background colors
  • Repeated borders, padding, and margins
  • Consecutive inline and block elements on the home page
  • Two floated advertisements
  • Three or more relative-positioned elements
  • Two or more absolute-positioned elements
